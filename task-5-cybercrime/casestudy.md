## UPI Fraud Case Study

UPI fraud is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into transferring money through digital payment apps. One common method involves sending fake payment requests or QR codes to the victim. The attacker may pretend to be a seller, customer support agent, or even a known contact.

In a typical situation, the victim receives a message asking them to scan a QR code to receive money. However, instead of receiving money, the victim unknowingly approves a payment. In some cases, fraudsters also send fake links that look like official banking websites to steal login details.

Students and young users are often targeted because they frequently use digital payment apps and may not be aware of such scams. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and misuse of personal information.

With the increase in online transactions, such fraud cases are becoming more common. Therefore, awareness and caution are very important to avoid such situations.
