## UPI Fraud Case Study

1. Introduction

UPI (Unified Payments Interface), developed by the National Payments Corporation of India, enables instant digital payments through apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm.
While it is fast and convenient, it is also targeted by fraudsters using social engineering techniques.


2. General Scenario

A user receives a call/message from an unknown person claiming to:

be a buyer, bank official, or customer support agent

The fraudster convinces the user to approve a UPI request or share sensitive information.

As a result, money is transferred from the user's account without their full understanding.


3. Common Methods Used by Fraudsters
   
  1. Collect Request Scam
Fraudster sends a “Request Money” notification
User enters UPI PIN thinking they are receiving money
Money gets debited instead

  2. Fake Customer Care
Fraudster pretends to be from bank or app support
Asks user to:
share OTP
install screen-sharing apps
Gains access and transfers money
  3. QR Code Scam
     Fraudster sends a QR code
Claims scanning will “receive money”
Actually, it initiates a payment
  4. Phishing Links
Fake websites or SMS links
Look similar to official apps
Used to steal login details


. How the Fraud Works (Simple Explanation)

UPI has two types of transactions:

Push → You send money
Pull → Someone requests money

👉 Fraud happens when:

User does not understand the difference
User authorizes a transaction using UPI PIN
5. Impact
Financial loss
Loss of trust in digital payments
Mental stress

6.Reporting Fraud

In India, fraud can be reported to:
